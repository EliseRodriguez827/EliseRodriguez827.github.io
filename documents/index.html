My GIS Experience

I interned with the GIS Section, Public Works Engineering Division, City of Rio Rancho, New Mexico. I gained skills in reading construction plans and as-builts, using ArcGIS Pro, digitizing, georeferencing, and interpreting aerial imagery. I also used these skills working with QGIS to produce an illustrative map for an archaeological site. Currently, I am collaborating on a project to produce an interactive map showing Amazon rainforest loss in Brazil, and the underlying social factors.

My Archaeology Experience


I worked as a volunteer on New Mexico State Historic Sites public archaeology projects including Dig! Kuaua, Dig! Guisewa, and Dig! Historia. I participated in excavation and artifact analysis.

I completed archaeological field school through the University of New Mexico, participating in pedestrian survey, mapping, and site preservation, including graffitti removal.

I organized a team of my field school classmates to present a poster at the Pecos Conference for Southwestern archaeology.
